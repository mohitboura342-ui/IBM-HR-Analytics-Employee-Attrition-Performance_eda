## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is one of the most significant challenges organizations face because replacing experienced employees is both expensive and time-consuming. This project performs a comprehensive **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance dataset to uncover the key factors influencing employee turnover.

The analysis transforms raw HR data into actionable business insights using Python-based data analysis and visualization techniques.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition patterns
- Identify the major factors associated with employee turnover
- Perform complete data cleaning and preprocessing
- Explore demographic and job-related characteristics
- Generate meaningful visualizations
- Extract actionable HR insights for decision-making

# 💡 Key Insights

Some important findings include:

- Employees working overtime tend to leave the organization more frequently.
- Lower job satisfaction is associated with higher attrition.
- Monthly income varies significantly across different job roles.
- Employees with fewer years at the company generally show higher turnover.
- Work-life balance appears to influence employee retention.
- Certain departments and job roles experience relatively higher attrition rates.
- Age and total working experience also contribute to employee retention patterns.
